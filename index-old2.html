An operator who's comfortable everywhere the work happens

I'm comfortable everywhere the work actually gets done — in sprint planning with engineers, in spreadsheets with finance, and in front of users trying to figure out what they really need. The thread that connects all of it is turning ambiguity into a plan, and a plan into measurable outcomes.

Frameworks & Methodologies

Structure behind the intuition

Every product I've built has been guided by structured frameworks — combining Agile delivery, design thinking, and data-backed prioritization to move work from concept → clarity → customer impact.

Agile & Scrum
  • Sprint planning, backlog grooming, and retrospectives with 3–5 cross-functional teams
  • Defined epics, user stories, and acceptance criteria; improved delivery time ~40%
  • Jira boards, burndown charts, and daily standups across distributed teams
Design Thinking
  • Empathize → define → ideate → prototype → test, for early-stage ideation
  • 600+ learner and 50+ healthcare-user interviews to extract actionable insight
  • Journey maps to convert friction points into product features
Lean Product Development
  • MVP-first roadmaps to validate hypotheses before scaling
  • Reduced time-to-market ~30% via continuous experimentation
  • A/B testing and analytics to prioritize validated needs

RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) — prioritized backlog at Great Learning & STC Health → +25% roadmap delivery efficiency.

ICE (Impact, Confidence, Ease) — fast feature tests in early SeeMe.Ai sprints → MVP in 3 weeks with validated engagement.

Eisenhower Matrix (Urgency vs. Importance) — balanced stakeholder urgency with roadmap focus → -40% cross-team escalations.

OKRs
  • Defined objectives for growth, adoption, and efficiency (e.g., "reduce onboarding 30%", "increase data accuracy 42%")
  • Aligned leadership, design, and engineering around measurable value
KPI tracking
  • Dashboards linking input → output → outcome metrics
  • Connected operational KPIs (efficiency, churn, response time) to revenue
North Star alignment
  • Retention Rate (SeeMe.Ai), Course Completion % (Great Learning) as guiding metrics
  • Evaluated every release against long-term business goals

Research → Ideation → Validation → Build → Launch → Measure → Iterate
